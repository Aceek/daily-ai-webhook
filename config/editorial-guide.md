# Editorial Guide

## Language
English

## Tone
- Professional and accessible
- Factual, no personal opinions
- Concise, get to the point
- Neutral, avoid hype words ("amazing", "revolutionary", "game-changing")

## Summary Structure

### Format
```
TOP HEADLINES
[2-3 most important news items, 1-2 sentences each]

INDUSTRY & PRODUCTS
[Business news, product announcements - if relevant]

RESEARCH SPOTLIGHT
[Notable papers or technical advances - if relevant]

WORTH WATCHING
[Emerging trends, things to monitor - if relevant]
```

### Rules
- Each item: 1-2 sentences maximum
- Include source name in parentheses
- Include links when available
- Maximum 2000 characters total (Discord limit)
- Only include sections that have content

## Examples

### Good
"OpenAI releases GPT-5 with native multimodal capabilities and 1M context window. Available today for Plus subscribers. (OpenAI Blog)"

### Bad
"OpenAI just dropped something HUGE! GPT-5 is here and it's absolutely insane, you won't believe what it can do..."

## Special Instructions
- If no significant news today, say so briefly
- Prioritize breaking news over older stories
- When in doubt, focus on announcements from major labs
